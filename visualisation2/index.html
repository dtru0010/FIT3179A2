The Australian Energy Market

With the complexities and dynamic growth of the Australian Energy Market system and its overall goals, it is important to highlight, analyse and review the production, distribution, and consumption of fuels. As such, with recent trends, the market has been prioritizing the Net Zero Plan which aims to decarbonize the economy providing improvements in energy efficiency and reduced greenhouse gas emissions. Additionally, it is important to compare the global differences to explore the approaches in energy challenges to inspire best practices and innovations to improve the overall system.


Energy Production & Consumption

In the Australian landscape, black coal is the leading fuel type being supplied as energy throughout history. This correlates to how Australia falls under the top 5 countries in coal production. However, with the economic shifts towards renewable energy, as seen in the steady growth of renewable energy production, black coal is beginning to see an accelerated decline to reach decarbonization. Interestingly, natural gas has seen a increasing shift in production due to the improvements of technology allowing for economic extraction from places that were disadvantageous.

On the other hand, Australia overall is predominantly reliant on oil and coal for energy consumption. Yet, with the decreasing demand of coal extraction and the reliancy of renewables, coal and renewables have an inverse correlation. With New South Wales, Victoria and Queensland being the highest consumers of coal, Tasmania has been net zero, consuming mainly renewables as a result of their investment towards hydroelectric power systems. Lacking coal reserves, Northern Territory is heavily reliant on gas to supply their needs due to their abundance of gas resources.


Energy Flow

It is important to understand the distribution flow of energy as it assists in seeking efficiency issues, creating knowledge towards improving the sustainability of sources, improving the economic planning for energy needs and costs, and assessing and mitigating any environmental impact in relation to the flow from production to destination. Australia is known to be a major asset in the global energy market due to their leading exports in coal and one of the top exports in gas as a result of their abundance of resources, placing 72% of their total resources towards exports.


Road to Net Zero

Working towards achieve the decarbonization of Australia, tracking the emission projection allows to analyse the progression so that proper measures can be made to stay on track with reaching the 2050 target. In tracking, there are two scenarios that are explored. The baseline scenario refers to the current policies and measures that are currently inplace, whereas with additional measures scenario builds upon the baseline showing the impacts with the inclusion of policies that have been announced but are in the design and consultation phase.

Australia's Energy Mix refers to the contributions of each fuel type to the consumption. Once dominated by fossil fuels like coal and gas, the landscape is beginning to shift towards renewable sources. As such, with the goal of doubling the emission reductions by 2030, the overall goal of being net zero by 2050 means the reliance of 82% of our energy mix to be in renewable energy. The exponential growth in renewables and rapid decline in coal has lead to 34% of our energy mix to be renewable and a deduction of coal by 36.89% from 2001 to 2023.


Global Comparison

Across the world, it is improtant to view how the share of energy consumption in each country comes from certain fuels. This provides insight into the contributions of energy reliance in each country and how the growth of each fuel has occured over time, exploring the sustainability and security of its energy system. Additionally, it will help assess the economic and environmental trade-offs that each country may face or reveal technological and policy insights as the energy mix can reflect the effectiveness. Overall, the differences assist in identifying trends, shifts in demand, and reveal the role of various fuels in shaping the global energy market.
